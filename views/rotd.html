Healthy Ramen Bowl Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoons reduced-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cups sliced mixed mushrooms (such as shiitake, cremini, and oyster)
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 servings ramen noodles
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Sriracha sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the chicken or vegetable broth, water, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Bring to a boil.
  2. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pot and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, bring a separate pot of water to a boil. Carefully add the eggs and cook for 7 minutes for slightly soft centers or 9 minutes for firmer yolks. Transfer the eggs to a bowl of ice water to cool, then peel and halve them.
  4. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water.
  5. To serve, divide the cooked ramen noodles among 4 bowls. Ladle the broth and mushrooms over the noodles. Top with baby spinach, sliced green onions, halved eggs, chopped cilantro, toasted sesame seeds, and Sriracha sauce (if desired).
  6. Enjoy your healthy ramen bowls!

Note: Feel free to customize this recipe by adding additional vegetables, proteins, or toppings to suit your taste.

Healthy Egg Burger

Ingredients:

  • 4 eggs
  • 4 burger buns
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped tomato
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • Butter or mayonnaise for spreading

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, beat the eggs. Add finely chopped onion, tomato, coriander leaves, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, chaat masala, and salt. Mix well to combine.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Pour a portion of the egg mixture in the pan to make a round-shaped omelette. Cook on both sides until golden brown. Repeat with the remaining egg mixture to make 4 omelettes.
  3. Toast the burger buns lightly on a pan or toaster.
  4. Spread butter or mayonnaise on the toasted buns.
  5. Place an omelette on the bottom half of each bun.
  6. Cover with the top half of the bun.
  7. Your Anda Burger Masala Fried Egg Burger is ready to serve.
